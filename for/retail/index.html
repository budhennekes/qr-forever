Why your store code should never expire

Most QR generators make dynamic codes: the code points at their server, which forwards shoppers to your page. That works until the free trial ends or you drop the subscription after the campaign wraps. Then the decal you paid to design, print, and install points at a dead redirect, usually with someone else's upsell on it.

A static code has the link baked into the pattern itself. Nobody can turn it off, including me. The window decal from last year's campaign still scans. The full story is in do QR codes expire, and the sizing math for a sidewalk scan is in the print size guide.

Questions shop owners ask

What size should the QR code be on a window decal?

People scan a window from 2 to 6 feet away, and the rough rule is scanning distance divided by 10. That puts most window decals at 3 to 6 inches wide. The print size guide has the full table.

Can the same code survive a sale or campaign change?

Yes. Point the code at a stable page you control, like yourstore.com/current-offer, and swap the page contents when the campaign changes. The decal stays on the glass and the code keeps working.

Will it scan through glass?

Yes, as long as there's good contrast and no heavy glare hitting the code. Dark code on a light background works best. Test-scan the installed decal from the sidewalk at a few times of day before you call it done.

Do these codes expire?

Static codes never expire. The link is encoded in the pattern itself, so there's no server in the middle and nothing to lapse. It works as long as the page it points to exists.

Do I need an account or a subscription?

No. The tool runs in your browser, the code is free, and there is nothing to renew. Your links and logo never leave your device.
